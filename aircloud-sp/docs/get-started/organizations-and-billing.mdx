This page summarizes the Organization/Project structure, member management, and the credits/billing model you’ll encounter when getting started with Aircloud.

## Organization and Project relationship

An **Organization** is the top-level administrative unit (team/company). A **Project** is the unit where you operate workloads. Air API and Air Container are both created and managed inside projects.

## Credits and payments

Aircloud requires credit top-ups and billing information depending on the resources and workloads you use within a project.

![Credits and billing screen](/docs/images/billing-and-credits.png)

<Info>
  - Usage is measured in **AU**, which is converted to KRW.
  - Use **Purchase AU** to top up credits in advance and operate cloud services within your available limit.
  - When credits are fully depleted, you will be notified via **Email Alert**. If you do not top up, workloads may be force-stopped.
  - To prevent forced stops, you can enable **Auto Topup**, register a card, and automatically purchase AU based on predefined rules.
</Info>

Even if credits appear optional during onboarding, we recommend confirming billing readiness before running real workloads.

![Purchase AU screen](/docs/images/purchase-au.png)

- Supported payment methods
  - Toss Payments (Korea, AU prepaid top-up)
  - PayPal (Global, AU prepaid top-up)
  - Stripe (Global, AU prepaid top-up and card storage for Auto Topup)
- If you register a card for Auto Topup, once your remaining credits drop below a threshold, AU will be automatically purchased using the selected card based on the predefined top-up amount.

## Operational recommendations

- Separate projects for API testing and production workloads based on their purpose.
- For stable organization operations, define roles and access boundaries for each member.
- To ensure continuous service operations, review usage and billing regularly.
- Confirm your credits and payment status before deployments.
